Wretched Depths

Play as Sam and fish to your hearts content. Sell fish you've caught, collect better gear, and use it to explore the depths of the mysterious lake, but be warned as no one knows what lies at the bottom. Wretched Depths is a PSX-style horror fishing game created by students at the University of Utah.

Wretched Depths is by far the biggest project I have worked on, with a team consisting of twenty-six students. The project was developed in Unity and is the most successful title I have worked on, with it going on to win the GDWC Summer Season Hobby Award, which can be seen here.

For my contribution to the project, I was tasked with implementing select events to help immerse the player in the game as well as add tension. This was done using a events system that had been created by another developer on the team. Along with adding these events to the game, I also worked to find and resolve bugs regarding the game's inventory system, including data persistance between opening and closing the inventory, and improving overall functionality of the system. Through this project I was able to make my first dive into coding in 3-D space through helping to initiate the developement of the AI for the fish.

Overall this was an incredibly enjoyable experience to work on such a massive (in terms of indie development) game and allowed me to learn a lot regarding the game development process. Screenshots from the final project can be seen below.
