Khestoria

Create a world of your own while playing as Talise, a peaceful witch who is on a mission to get to the big city. Enjoy learning the concepts of chemistry by farming for rare ingredients and creating new concoctions using your cauldron in an effort to get the local train up and running again. Khestoria is a pixel-art farming simulator with the intent of teaching chemistry.
Khestoria was my first venture into creating a real gaming experience and is one of my favorite projects that I have worked on. For this, I worked on a very small team of three, using Gamemaker Studio 2.

With such a small team, we were all given opportunities to take on a lot of work as well as wear many hats throughout development. For my contribution, I decided to take up a lot of the programming with some of the art, as it seemed to be good experience for someone interested in tech-art. To start off with, I worked to develop the home screen along with creating sprite-sheets for the enemy slimes. I also worked to add item drops to slimes upon dying and collision detection along with room transitions for a smoother player experience. I implemented UI Elements as well including the look and functionaly of the clock, day-time dial, health, and stamina of the player.

Lastly, I used the opportunity to learn to implment some standard player systems. These systems include a day/night cycle that implements shaders based on the time of day and allow for tracking which allows for in-game crops to grow. The second system created is a dialogue system that allows for multiple responses and branching dialogue. Lastly, I worked to implement the enemy AI as well as a combat system inluding player feedback whenever damage is taken or dealt.

Overall, this was my favorite project I have worked on so far. Having a small team allowed for creative flexibility and allowed all group members to take on work that they were interested in. Below are Screenshots from the project followed by the release trailer.
